About Me

Short Bio

I’m someone who believes that growth happens when curiosity meets consistency. My journey into tech started with nothing but a laptop, a bootcamp, a few tutorials, and an unshakable desire to create something meaningful. I remember the first time I built a small webpage, although it wasn’t perfect, but it sparked something in me. Since then, I’ve been on an adventure of learning, unlearning, and building. Now, as a frontend developer, I enjoy turning ideas into interactive, user-friendly experiences. I love the feeling of bringing design to life, solving tricky problems, and watching my skills evolve with every new challenge. Beyond code, I’m driven by connection, by the stories, collaborations, and shared passion that make tech such a vibrant space to grow in.

My Goals in This Program

My goal in the HNG program is to go beyond just learning, I want to grow through experience and build meaningful connections with people who share the same hunger for improvement. I see this program as a bridge between what I’ve already learnt and the professional developer I aspire to become. I want to work on real projects that push me outside my comfort zone, collaborate with talented teammates, and learn how to navigate challenges in a team environment. I believe growth happens in community, through feedback, collaboration, and shared problem-solving. So while I aim to sharpen my technical skills, I also want to form bonds with people who inspire me, challenge me, and maybe even walk alongside me long after HNG ends.

Areas of Growth

If I’m being honest, I sometimes struggle with confidence when I’m stepping into something unfamiliar, like a new technology or a complex project that feels bigger than what I’ve handled before. There are moments I question if I’m good enough or if I belong in certain spaces where everyone seems to know more. But over time, I’ve learned that it’s okay to feel uncertain. Confidence, for me, is no longer about having all the answers, it’s about staying curious enough to find them. I’m learning to give myself grace, to ask questions without fear, and to see mistakes as proof that I’m trying. I may not always feel confident, but I’m always willing to grow and that, I’ve realized, is a quiet kind of courage.

Note To My Future Self

Hey future me, I hope you’re smiling right now, not because everything is perfect, but because you remember where it all started. You remember the nights you sat staring at error messages that made no sense. You remember doubting whether you were even cut out for this tech journey. You remember that feeling of staring at a blank VS Code window, unsure how to begin, but you began anyway. Back then, every little win mattered. The first “Hello World.” The first time your CSS grid finally aligned. The first project that actually worked the way you imagined it would. You didn’t know it then, but every late night debugging session, every YouTube tutorial you replayed a hundred times, every small improvement, they were shaping you into the developer you are today. So if life ever gets busy and you start to forget the “why,” pause and breathe. Remember that your journey wasn’t just about becoming a great developer, it was about becoming a person who never gave up on growth. You once prayed to be where you are now. You once dreamed of building things that make people’s lives easier. So don’t lose that fire, that curiosity, that hunger to learn. Celebrate the progress, no matter how small. Stay humble, stay kind, and keep showing up, even on the hard days. Because one day, you’ll look back again… and smile even wider.

Extra Thoughts

If there’s one thing I’ve learned, it’s that progress rarely feels like progress when you’re in the middle of it. It’s easy to look around and feel behind, but growth is often invisible until one day you look back and realize how far you’ve come. I joined this journey not just to build skills, but to build resilience, to learn how to keep showing up even when things get hard. I’m excited, nervous, and hopeful all at once. And if I could leave a note to myself for this program, it would simply say: Be open. Be kind. Learn loudly. Fail gracefully. Keep building. Because every experience, whether it’s a success or a struggle, is another line of code in the story I’m writing for myself.
